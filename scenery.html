Landmarks of the Ancient Capital

Must-Visit Attractions

Explore the legendary landmarks that define Kaifeng's timeless beauty

Millennium City Park

Based on Zhang Zeduan's masterpiece "Along the River During the Qingming Festival," this 33-hectare park recreates the vibrant life of the Northern Song capital with over 400 reconstructed buildings, traditional boats on winding waterways, and daily cultural performances that bring the ancient world to life.

Tianbo Yang Manor

Rebuilt in 1994, this magnificent estate honors the legendary Yang family, national heroes of the Northern Song Dynasty who defended the empire with unwavering loyalty across multiple generations.

Zhuxian Town Yue Fei Temple

One of the four great Yue Fei Temples in China, this historic shrine was first built in 1478 at Zhuxian Town, where the legendary general achieved his greatest military victories.

Baozhu Temple

A serene Buddhist temple featuring a unique blend of Song and Qing Dynasty architectural styles, housing a magnificent jade reclining Buddha and ancient scriptures donated from Taiwan.

Martial Arts City

Built on the site of a former national forest park, this expansive theme park stages over 60 daily live performances inspired by classic martial arts novels including "Water Margin" and "Legend of the Condor Heroes."

The Iron Pagoda

Kaifeng's most iconic landmark, the Iron Pagoda (Kaibao Temple Pagoda) was built in 1049 AD during the Northern Song Dynasty. Standing 55 meters tall, it features over 50 kinds of exquisite glazed tile decorations depicting Buddhist stories, making it one of China's finest examples of Song Dynasty architecture.

Dragon Pavilion

Perched atop a massive 13-meter-high platform that once formed the heart of the Song Dynasty imperial palace, the Dragon Pavilion offers panoramic views of Kaifeng and its beautiful surrounding lakes.

Daxiangguo Temple

One of China's ten most famous Buddhist temples, Daxiangguo Temple was originally built in 555 AD during the Northern Qi Dynasty and was reconstructed during the Tang Dynasty to its current grand scale.
